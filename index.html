MoMA

Based on this Guerrilla Girls poster, we decided to investigate the gender distribution of artists exhibited at major museums. We asked the question "Is there a lack female representation at MoMA?" From this, stemmed a larger question of what communities are over- and underrepresented at the Museum of Modern Art in NYC.

To answer this, we looked at a Kaggle dataset of all artworks exhibited by the MoMA up to approximately 2017 accompanied by various other metadata. We were interested in understanding representation of different communities at the museum.

Below, our exploration of this topic. Enjoy.

Concluding Remarks

After creating all of the visualizations and exploring the dataset we had on MoMA artwork acquisition, we have seen a tendency toward acquiring works of male artists more than female artists. We already knew that the number of works of male artists would be higher than the number of works of female artists. We have confirmed our overall bias with the bar charts. Later, we have also seen this trend existing overtime in our time-series graph. The most valuable inference from the time-series graph was the constant gap between male and female artists purchased by the museum, maintained through purchasing spikes, such as the reconstruction during the 1960s. These tendencies were an indicator of a bias toward male artists in MoMA.

Analyzing the demographics of the artists, we were able to see a variety of nationalities but a vast majority were European and US American. Lastly, we looked at male and female artwork acquisition for each type of artwork and observed the disparity in each category, such as painting. If we had more time, it would be interesting to contrast artwork types with nationality over the years, or other intersections between our demographic data that allow us to have a narrower understanding of the communities favored by MoMA curators.
